About

I am a junior in Computer Science at the Honors College of Hangzhou Dianzi University, with a record of merit-based scholarships. My current research focuses on computer vision and 3D reconstruction, particularly 3D Gaussian Splatting.

My broader interests extend to world models for embodied spatial reasoning, and to AI for social science—an area I approach from a long-standing immersion in classical Chinese literature, with interests in computational philology, the decipherment of ancient scripts, and the cultural-historical analysis of textual traditions.

V

Research & Experience

Sep 2024 — Mar 2026

Computer Vision Research Project

Independent Research · First author
Applied 3D Gaussian Splatting to sparse-view CT reconstruction and 3D-consistent multi-organ segmentation, addressing the clinical trade-off between radiation dose and volumetric accuracy. Built an SDF-based Gaussian segmentation framework—converting organ masks into signed distance fields and assigning covariance-aware soft labels via trilinear SDF sampling—reaching a mean Dice of 0.927, well above the nnU-Net baseline (0.84).
2023 — Present

Education Research: Classical Chinese Education & Student Development

Independent Research · Lead
A mixed-methods study—surveys paired with semi-structured interviews—tracking 40+ former classmates across the six years after primary school, examining how a classics-centered primary curriculum shaped their later academic performance, character, and cultural disposition. Designed the questionnaire and interview protocol and stratified participants into three cohorts (the domestic gaokao track; domestic non-gaokao and international tracks; and overseas study) to compare this classical-education model against standard compulsory education—across Chinese-language attainment, literary writing, social adjustment, and reasoning and aesthetic sensibility. Analysis ongoing.

Computer Graphics Project (UC Berkeley CS184)

UC Berkeley · Project lead
Led an end-to-end real-time VR gaming pipeline built on 3D Gaussian Splatting: multi-view scene capture, camera-pose extraction, COLMAP-based Structure-from-Motion, and Gaussian scene reconstruction for photorealistic novel-view synthesis. Extended Nerfstudio's Splatfacto for online scene adaptation—incrementally updating exported Gaussians in ~0.5 s for interactive VR without full retraining. Selected for the CS184 Showcase.

Computer Systems & Architecture Projects

Course Project
Built a von Neumann-style computer-system simulator in C based on the RV32IM ISA—core execution, trap and exception handling, context switching, system calls, user-program loading, and a simple file system supporting batch execution on Nanos-lite. Designed and debugged a five-stage pipelined RV64 CPU in Chisel (IF/ID/EX/MEM/WB) with units for ALU operations, branching, memory access, multiplication/division, and CSR handling; implemented data forwarding, load-use hazard detection, CSR read/write, and exception/interrupt handling to improve pipeline correctness and efficiency.
